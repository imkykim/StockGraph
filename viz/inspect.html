How to read this graph

Circle size — how often the entity is mentioned in the reports.

Circle color — company classification, switchable above. Value-chain role is derived from supply-edge directions across all weeks (mostly supplies → upstream; mostly buys → downstream; both → integrator; neither → peripheral). Industry is a curated GICS-style bucket.

Line width — combined weight of every record between the two entities, per direction. Each record's weight = (mentions ÷ max mentions) × contract scale. Contract scale is the reported quantity (GW, 亿USD, K units, WPM…), log-scaled and normalized within its own unit family — so it ranks deal sizes relatively, not in absolute dollars. Records with no quantity get a 0.1 floor. The thickest lines are big deals mentioned repeatedly.

Line opacity — extraction confidence.

Line style — solid = SUPPLIES / CONTRACTS_WITH, dashed = COMPETES_WITH, dotted = CO_MENTION.

Arrow — direction of the relation (e.g. supplier → customer).

Click a line — see its individual records with dates, quantities and evidence; click a node for all its relationships.

Isolate — a node's detail panel offers “⊙ Isolate neighborhood”: only that entity, its direct partners and the links among them stay. Exit with the chip above the stats or Esc.
